# CarePath Case Study #6

## Monica (CNA / Home Health Caregiver)

## 1. Participant Profile

- Role: CNA / home health caregiver
- Context: Limited but useful insight from someone supporting patients in home and caregiving settings
- Segment relevance: Caregiver perspective on transportation barriers and missed appointments

## 2. Context of the Conversation

- Method: Remote text-based exchange
- Selection rationale: Available caregiver perspective while in-person outreach was not possible
- Prompt: Asked whether she had witnessed anyone missing appointments due to transportation barriers

## 3. Problem Identified (User Voice)

Direct quotes from participant:

- "Sometimes"
- "Unreliable resources"

Observed pattern:

- Transportation barriers do show up in caregiving settings
- Insight was limited and non-specific
- No example of crisis or severe downstream harm was reported

## 4. Impact of the Problem

- Logistical impact: Some missed appointments likely linked to unreliable transportation resources
- Medical impact: No health crisis or major escalation was reported
- Emotional impact: Limited detail available
- Financial impact: Not discussed

## 5. Reaction to CarePath Concept

- No detailed reaction captured
- Response confirms the issue exists, but the evidence is shallow

## 6. Key Insights Extracted

- Caregivers still see transportation barriers in real settings
- Unreliable transportation resources remain a recurring theme
- This interview is useful as a weak signal, not a strong validation point

## 7. Validation Signal Strength

- Weak

Justification:

- The participant confirmed the issue exists
- However, the feedback was brief, lacked examples, and did not show severity or downstream harm

## 8. How This Informs the Pilot

- Continue collecting caregiver perspectives, but do not treat this interview as a decisive signal
- Use it as supporting evidence that transportation unreliability is still present in caregiving contexts
- Prioritize stronger examples before changing the pilot based on this segment alone
